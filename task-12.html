White light dispersion through a triangular prism is simulated on a coordinate grid. An incident ray at angle θ enters the prism and refracts, splitting into multiple wavelengths due to crown glass’ wavelength-dependent refractive index.
The prism’s apex angle α controls the internal refraction geometry and the spread of the emerging spectrum. Each wavelength exits at a different angle, forming a rainbow-like dispersion.
Transmission angle θt of light exiting a triangular prism is plotted against the incidence angle θi for a given input frequency f and prism apex angle α .
Refraction varies with incidence angle and frequency, as determined by the material’s refractive index via the Sellmeier equation. Curve colours represent their corresponding colours within the visible spectrum.
The vertical dashed line indicates the minimum incidence angle θmin for which light is refracted through the prism rather than totally internally reflected.
Deflection angle δ of a light ray passing through a triangular prism is plotted against the incidence angle θi for a given input frequency f and prism apex angle α .
The deflection angle depends on the prism geometry and the frequency-dependent refractive index, modeled by the Sellmeier equation. Curve colours represent their corresponding colours within the visible spectrum.
The vertical dashed line indicates the minimum incidence angle θmin for which light is refracted through the prism rather than totally internally reflected.
