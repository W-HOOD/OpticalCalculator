This ray diagram simulates myopia, also known as short-sightedness. Without correction, parallel rays focus in front of the retina, causing the blur experienced in shortsighted vision. When the concave correction lens is applied, incoming rays are bent outward, shifting the focal point rearward so they converge precisely on the retina.
This ray diagram simulates hyperopia, also known as long-sightedness. Without correction, parallel rays focus behind the retina, causing the blur experienced in farsighted vision. When the convex correction lens is applied, incoming rays are bent inward, shifting the focal point forward so they converge precisely on the retina.
